Software Requirements Specification (SRS) 
Project: HURE Care — Small/Medium Hospital System (≤20 Beds) 
Version: 1.0 
Date: 19 Feb 2026 
 
1. Purpose 
This document defines the functional and non-functional requirements for HURE Care, a 
hospital system designed for small/medium facilities (up to 20 beds) that need strong 
OPD + Inpatient workflows, specialty clinics (e.g., Dental, Gyna), and billing/insurance 
readiness, including assistive AI features. 
 
2. Goals and Objectives 
Primary Goals 
1. Provide a complete OPD workflow: registration → triage → consultation → orders → 
pharmacy → billing → close visit. 
2. Provide a complete Inpatient workflow: admission → bed management → ward care 
→ orders → billing → discharge. 
3. Make documentation safer and faster using assistive AI (clinician review + sign 
required). 
4. Make billing insurance-claims ready (manual submission now, integrations later). 
5. Ensure the system is audit-safe, access-controlled, and practical for Kenya/East 
Africa operations. 
 
3. Scope 
In Scope (Core Modules) 
• User & role management with permissions 
• Patient registration and demographics 
• OPD visits + triage + queue management

4. Users and Roles 
Standard Roles (Minimum) 
1. System Admin: configuration, users, permissions, full reporting, audit access. 
2. Receptionist: register patients, create OPD visits, manage appointments/queue. 
3. Nurse (Triage/OPD): vitals, triage notes, route patients. 
4. Clinician/Doctor: clinical notes, orders, prescriptions, admit/discharge. 
5. Dentist: procedures + prescriptions. 
6. Gyna Clinician: gyna notes + follow-ups. 
7. Ward Nurse (Inpatient): ward notes, vitals round, medication administration record 
(MAR). 
8. Lab Technician: samples, test processing, results entry. 
9. Imaging Staff: imaging request fulfillment + report attachment. 
10. Pharmacist/Dispenser: dispensing + stock deductions.

11. Cashier/Billing Officer: invoices, receipts, claim draft prep, daily close. 
Permission Rules (High-level) 
• Clinical signing is only allowed for clinicians/dentists/gyna roles. 
• Billing edits are restricted to billing staff. 
• Admin/Employer sees incomplete note status after 24h but not note content 
(unless explicitly granted). 
 
5. Key Workflows 
5.1 OPD Workflow (Day/Outpatient) 
1. Patient arrives → receptionist searches/creates patient. 
2. Receptionist creates OPD visit (clinic type, payer type) → patient placed in queue. 
3. Nurse triage captures vitals + chief complaint → routes to clinician queue. 
4. Clinician consults, documents (SOAP), orders labs/imaging, prescribes. 
5. Patient proceeds to lab/imaging/pharmacy as needed. 
6. Billing auto-aggregates services + dispensed meds → cashier receives 
payment/creates insurance bill. 
7. Visit closed with summary and follow-up appointment (if needed). 
5.2 Inpatient Workflow (≤20 Beds) 
1. Clinician admits patient from OPD or direct admission. 
2. Ward nurse completes admission intake checklist and starts care notes. 
3. Ward rounds: clinician updates progress notes and orders; nurse records vitals and 
MAR. 
4. Billing accumulates bed-days + services + pharmacy/labs. 
5. Clinician discharges: discharge summary + final prescriptions + follow-up. 
6. Bed is released and inpatient episode closed.

6. Functional Requirements 
6.1 Patient Registration & Demographics 
• System shall create and manage patient master records: 
o Name, gender, DOB/age, phone(s), address/town, next of kin, allergies, 
chronic conditions, email, id number 
o Insurance details (scheme, member number, principal, payer type) 
• System shall allow fast patient search by phone, ID, or name. 
• System shall store attachments (referrals, IDs, prior reports). 
6.2 Appointments + Confirmation Badges 
• System shall support appointment scheduling by clinic and provider. 
• Appointment status shall include: Scheduled, Confirmed, Cancelled, No-show, 
Completed. 
• System shall automatically set booking type: 
o SD (Same Day) if created date equals appointment date 
o ADV (Advance) otherwise 
• System shall display confirmation badges: 
o C (Confirmed), NC (Not confirmed), LM (Left message) 
• System shall log confirmation attempts (time, method, staff). 
6.3 OPD Visit + Queue Management 
• System shall create visits and assign them to clinics/providers. 
• System shall display real-time queues by clinic/provider. 
• System shall track visit progression statuses (e.g., waiting triage, in consultation, 
sent to lab). 
6.4 Triage (Nursing) 
• System shall capture vitals: BP , pulse, temp, weight, height, SpO2 (optional). 
• System shall allow triage notes and urgency flags. 
 
5 
 
6.5 Clinical Notes (SOAP) + Assistive AI (Clinician Safe) 
• System shall allow clinical notes via Text-only or Audio → Transcript. 
• If Audio mode is used: 
o transcript shall appear immediately after recording 
o clinician shall edit transcript 
o clinician must click Mark transcript reviewed before Save & Sign 
• System shall support: 
o generating SOAP from transcript 
o parsing single note into SOAP fields 
o rewriting for clarity (assist only; no meaning change) 
• Notes shall support: 
o Save Draft 
o Save & Sign (final) 
• Notes shall display origin label: Created via Audio or Text-only. 
6.6 ICD-10 Suggestions (Assistive Only) 
• System shall provide AI-suggested ICD-10 codes for clinician review. 
• System shall require clinician manual selection; no auto assignment. 
• System shall store selected ICD-10 codes linked to the signed note. 
6.7 Incomplete Notes Control (End-of-Day Closure) 
• System shall show clinicians same-day reminders: 
o count of incomplete notes 
o list of encounters with “No note started” or “Draft not signed” 
• Admin/Employer shall see completion status only after a 24-hour grace period. 
• Admin view shall include aging buckets: 24–48h, 48–72h, 72h+. 
6.8 Orders: Lab / Imaging (Basic) 
• System shall allow clinicians to order lab tests and imaging. 
6 
 
• Lab/imaging shall update status: ordered → collected/in progress → results ready. 
• Results shall appear in the encounter and patient history. 
• Critical results flag is optional but recommended. 
6.9 Pharmacy (OPD + Inpatient Dispensing) 
• System shall receive prescriptions electronically from clinicians. 
• System shall support dispensing: 
o full, partial, or unavailable 
• System shall reduce stock quantities upon dispensing (basic inventory). 
• System shall link dispensed items to billing. 
6.10 Inpatient: Beds + Ward Notes + Discharge 
• System shall manage wards and beds (availability, assignment, release). 
• System shall record: 
o admission reason/diagnosis 
o ward nursing notes and vitals rounds 
o medication administration record (MAR) 
• System shall generate discharge summary and discharge prescription. 
6.11 Referral Notes (AI-Assisted, Editable, Downloadable) 
• System shall support a new note type: Referral Note. 
• System shall generate referral note from a selected visit note source: 
o default: most recent signed SOAP in that visit 
o fallback: draft with warning if no signed note exists 
• Referral note output shall support: 
o Save Draft / Save & Sign 
o Download PDF , Print, Copy email text 
• System shall store a PDF snapshot for audit and history. 
6.12 Patient Intake Forms (Secure Link, Pending Review) 
7 
 
• System shall allow staff to send intake forms via secure link: 
o link expires in 24 hours 
o patient verifies identity using last 4 digits of phone 
• Submissions shall be marked Pending Review. 
• Staff shall review and Accept or Reject. 
• Accepting insurance form updates Demographics (single source of truth). 
• System shall store PDF snapshot of submitted form for audit. 
6.13 Billing + Insurance Claims-Ready 
• System shall generate invoices automatically from services, procedures, labs, and 
dispensed meds. 
• System shall support payments: card, mobile money (for accountability only no 
cash) 
• If payer is Insurance: 
o show insurance summary read-only from Demographics 
o allow Prepare Insurance Claim 
• System shall create Claim Draft: 
o insurance snapshot 
o selected ICD-10 list 
o itemized bill snapshot 
o attachments references 
• System shall generate a Claim Pack PDF for manual submission.

8. Non-Functional Requirements 
Security & Privacy 
• Role-based access control (RBAC) 
• Automatic screen warning at 90 seconds inactivity; lock at 2 minutes 
• Track “last accessed” and “last edited” 
• Data encryption at rest and in transit (TLS) 
• Access logs and audit logs must be immutable (append-only) 
Clinician Safety 
• AI outputs are always suggestions; clinician reviews + signs final record 
• Block signing if transcript not reviewed (for audio notes) 
• Allow “edit after sign” only with reason + audit trail 
Performance 
9 
 
• OPD queue pages must load quickly (<3 seconds typical). 
• Search and check-in should be optimized for busy front desk usage. 
Reliability 
• Automatic backups 
• Graceful handling of unstable internet (recommended caching/retry strategy) 
 
9. Reporting Requirements (Minimum) 
• Daily OPD attendance 
• Revenue by day/service/provider 
• Appointment status summary (confirmed/cancelled/no-show) 
• Inpatient occupancy (beds used vs available) 
• Incomplete notes report (admin view after 24h) 
• Claims list report and download claim pack 
 
10. Acceptance Criteria (Client Verification) 
The system will be accepted when: 
1. Clinician cannot Save & Sign an audio note without marking transcript reviewed. 
2. Notes clearly show “Created via Audio” vs “Text-only” . 
3. ICD-10 suggestions require manual selection (no auto-assign). 
4. Providers see same-day incomplete notes; employers/admin see only after 24h and 
without note content. 
5. Referral notes can be generated from selected visits, saved, signed, and 
downloaded as PDF . 
6. Appointment badges SD/ADV and C/NC/LM render and update correctly with audit 
logs. 
7. Intake form links expire in 24h; last-4 phone verification works; submissions follow 
Pending Review → Accept/Reject flow. 
10 
 
8. Claim drafts can be created from bills; claim pack PDF downloads; claim snapshots 
do not change when demographics is updated later. 
9. Audit logs exist for all major clinical, billing, and claims actions. 
 
11. Implementation Phases (Recommended) 
Phase 1: Core OPD + Billing 
• Users/Roles, Patient registration, OPD visits, Triage, Consultation, Pharmacy, Billing, 
basic reports 
Phase 2: Inpatient 
• Admissions, beds, ward notes, MAR, discharge, inpatient billing 
Phase 3: AI + Workflow Enhancements 
• AI SOAP + ICD-10, end-of-day incomplete notes, referral note generation + PDF 
Phase 4: Claims-Ready + Intake Forms 
• intake forms + pending review, claim drafts + claim pack, appointment badges + 
reminders (messaging connectors later)
